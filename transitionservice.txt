### ✅ **Recommended to have State Transition Services**

| Entity    | Statuses                                                                 | Why a Service is Needed                                                                                             |
|-----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Bukid** | `active`, `inactive`, `archived`                                         | Changing status may affect pitaks, assignments, and reports. E.g., archiving a bukid should prevent new pitaks.    |
| **Pitak** | `active`, `inactive`, `completed`                                        | When a pitak is completed, it may trigger final payments or affect utilization stats. Inactivation may block assignments. |
| **Session**| `active`, `closed`, `archived`                                          | Closing or archiving a session should freeze all related data (assignments, payments, debts) and possibly generate reports. |
| **Debt**  | `pending`, `partially_paid`, `paid`, `cancelled`, `overdue`              | Status changes affect worker balance, may trigger interest calculations, or affect payment allocation.              |
| **Payment**| `pending`, `processing`, `completed`, `cancelled`, `partially_paid`     | Completing a payment could reduce debts, update worker earnings, and trigger notifications.                         |
| **Worker**| `active`, `inactive`, `on-leave`, `terminated`                           | Status changes may affect assignment eligibility, debt collection, and payment processing.                          |

### 🧠 **Implementation Notes**

- **Keep it simple at first**: Start with services for entities where status changes have immediate, critical side effects (e.g., **Payment** → reduce debt, **Debt** → update worker balance). You can add others later.
- **Reuse the pattern**: Each service should have methods like `onActivate`, `onComplete`, `onCancel`, etc., depending on the statuses. The subscriber will detect status changes and call the appropriate method.
- **Leverage settings**: For example, when a `Payment` is completed, you may want to check settings like `auto_apply_debt_payment` before deducting debt.
